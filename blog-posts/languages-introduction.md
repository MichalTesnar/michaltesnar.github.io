# Why I Will Teach You Languages
## Why am I sharing this?
As I am opening a new chapter of my life, I am more actively thinking who I want to be. What do I want to mean to other people, and what I want to mean for myself. And more and more I am figuring out, that I just want to mean something.

The most important of people on this planet are the ones who have something to give. An example of that is wealthy people, what makes landlord so rich is the fact that they have a facility to share. Another example, can be politician. What makes them important, is that they control policies.

But there is another aspect to being important. That is to be sharing what you have with others. If you keep it for yourself, it surely remains yours, but you do not exchange it for the importance in the eyes of other people. To become important is to share with others the things that you have. That can be anything, information, skill, power, money or just positive attitude. 

And, so I though a unique talent I have. Or at least an interest. And I came up with one thing: I like languages. What makes my interest unique is that I am not honing any certificates in those, all I do is just learn to communicate, so that I can connect with other people. That is my interest. I love to learn them, I love to speak them. And that is the talent I decided I will share with the world.
## So, what is a language?
I think my success and enjoyment of learning languages has stemmed from a different standpoint about languages. I think language, more than anything else, it is a tool. It is a tool that helps you express your thoughts. Originally, developed to communicate information to other humans, language has taken on many purposes. It serves us to express feelings in the form of art, it helps us store knowledge, it helps us to command other people. But in essence of all those things, it is just a tool. It just matters how we use it. It is nothing of significance of its own, it must be used in one way or another.

School education ends up teaching us grammar and categories of literature, somehow giving the false idea that those things are what language is. Set of prescriptive rules, of how to do things. But that is not what it is, you can do anything with is as long as it serves your purpose. You can invent new world, and people will understand them. It is about relating to other people, more than anything else.
## Why do you need a language?
About relating to people. You see, naturally, languages are learned in some circumstances, when one really needs them. Many people learn a language when they relocate, or when they fall in love. But you can reverse this motivation. If you want to use this tool, that people call language. You need a strong reason to connect.

So go give yourself a reason. A job, a place, a person, a salary, a business, an impression. 

You can do it. Just give yourself a reason, and start pounding that language hammer on to that word steel. And it will work, without grammar, without any formal knowledge whatsoever.

You have heard of those people who say "Yeah, I do not speak a word of the language, but that day when I need to go to hospital in Zambia, I was fluent in all the dialects of the local language." The person just needed a strong reason to connect with the other people, the necessity made them communicate, made them use that language tool.

I love this tool, so I would like to tell you more about it.
