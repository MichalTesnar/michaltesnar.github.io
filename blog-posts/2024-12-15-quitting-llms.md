# Why I am Quitting ChatGPT: And Why You Should Too
*Yes, I know it is a clickbait title, but before you call me out for being against progress, please read at least a bit to get what I am trying to express.*

### Sir, Just One Quick Question
Let me ask you one! Try to answer and then read on.

*What is the population of Poland?*

Unless you are one of the thirty six million people that live there, you mind have a bit of a difficult time answering. Or maybe not. Because you have the internet, just opening a new tab and making typing it out, you get a response within microseconds. This observation is certainly has been made before, [access to technology shortens our attention spans](https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2019/oct/03/before-the-internet-broke-my-attention-span-i-read-books-compulsively-now-it-takes-willpower) and  [generally changes the way we interact with the internet](https://www.cmhs-sarasota.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Is-Google-Making-Us-Stupid_Carr.pdf). In fact, I have not even read the  articles I linked, I just skimmed it to verify that it fits my narrative.

In recent years, we can easily count examples of technology that have changed the way we think. With every powerful tool we get quick and robust access to more information. However, this quick gain makes us also lose a skill:

- The first example must be *pocket calculators*. Each computer nowadays has one implemented in it, so none of us have to make errors when doing mental math. The fact that an occupation of [a computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_(occupation)) has as that has fully been taken over by the machine, name of which is very fittingly chosen given the same name.

- My favorite example are Google Maps. It goes without saying that if I am in a new city without a guide (be it friends, family or a tour guide), I have to pay way more attention myself and remember the topology a lot better. However, [research shows](https://www.nature.com/articles/ncomms14652) that Google Maps have the same effect on you. Easy access to navigation prevents you from creating mental representation of a city, it is simply not necessary, as you always know whether the next step should be right or left.

- My mom would always make fun of me, that I do not know how the alphabet goes. That is not cause I have a disorder, or my mom is mean, but simply because my mom has studied languages at a university during times when computers were only starting and online dictionaries were not a thing. Knowing the alphabet intuitively was super useful to speed up lookup. Nowadays, it is useless.

But now we have a new tool entering the game...
### The Almighty
*For the purposes of this text, large languages models and ChatGPT are used interchangeably.*

It is so easy to talk to. You do not even need an account, just type `chatgpt.com` into our browser and voila, you can start talking. What is more, it is integrated in just about every tool you can use nowadays with a nice little chat window function. It is truly remarkable piece of technology, which condenses real world knowledge in written form in front of you just on demand. It is ready there for you to solve any problem... 

...and so I use it frequently.

It is Monday evening. The deadline is at midnight. I really cannot get this program to work. "The network is JUST NOT TRAINING. WHY???" I am staring at it and I do not know why. I copy paste the code into ChatGPT, asking for help. It points out to some error in code. I fix them. Still not working. Another try. Then I try other models, Gemini from Google, Claude, Grok... I am going back and forth... Still cannot find the error.

And that has become a common experience in my daily life. Similar situations with mathematical exercises where I get stuck, same for theoretical quizzes that I cannot pass on course websites. You see, I study Computer Science and Mathematics: both very much symbol based problems, which ChatGPT is good at. With a few click and no costs, I can unleash the power of 1.8 trillion parameters of thinking to get myself to the answer. However, this causes a problem.

1. I am lazy to struggle. I do not learn, I just generate. That is how we people work. It is how universe works. Least resistance. We optimize for the current environment. But easy come, easy go.
2. I do not ask for help from people. I do not expose my stupidity to other people. This further enhances impostor syndrome. I get more familiar with the truth, even if it is wrong. I do not talk to others.
3. I just do what gets me there, I do not create mental representations. LLMs just get me direclty to the output.

In the same way a child cries out help to mom, when cannot work it out, I have become like this with LLM.

We are loosing, not developing skills. That is fine, but the point that I am trying to make. Are we losing the skill of reasoning? Go back, read the question!

I am also a student, and I am here to learn. If I want to learn those skills I should do the work.
I have unninstalled copilot, and now I am using more intentionally. It is important that we know how we interact with the tool, and what intention we have for using it.

Is it something that is useless, typo, or a new library, that would be fine to skip? Then use LLMs. Is is a new software architecture that I am trying to learn? Maybe talk to ChatGPT, but do not let it generate my code. Funnily
